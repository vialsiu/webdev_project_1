Statistics on Sleep

1. Global Insomnia Statistics in 2022 and 2023

[Graph 1 and Graph 2]

How often do US adults struggle to fall asleep in 2022?

• As many as 1 in 5 adults in the USA (22%) struggle to fall asleep every single night.
• On top of that, more than a third of US adults struggle to fall asleep at least once a week (36%).
• Half of US adults experience insomnia once a month or more (50%).
• 43% of adults in the US do not experience insomnia.

2. 53 Sleep Statistics: What Percentage of the Population Is Sleep Deprived?

[Graph 4 and Graph 4]

• 35% of the US population get less than the recommended seven hours of sleep each night.
• The US economy loses $411 billion annually due to sleep deprivation.
• Up to 70 million adults in the US have a sleep disorder.
• Only 3% of teenagers get the recommended 8-10 hours of sleep each night.

3. U.S. News and World Report Sleep Quality Survey 2022

[Graph 5 and Graph 8]

Roughly one-third of Americans are not currently getting quality sleep, according to our analysis. Nearly 20% of people said they rarely or never wake up well rested and 16% of people say they only feel well rested a few times a month. Only 13% of people say they usually wake up every day feeling well-rested.

The lack of sleep leaves many Americans ready to trade other pleasures for a high-quality snooze. Over 38% of people said they would give up eating out for a month in exchange for perfect sleep. Nearly 28% of people would give up seeing friends for a month, and roughly 27% of Americans would give up TV and movies in order to find that perfect night's sleep.

Most Americans recognize that they have some bad habits that interfere with their sleep. Over 40% of people say they change their bedtimes too often, and 33% of people say they eat too much before going to sleep. There's evidence that eating or drinking within one hour of bedtime can increase your chances of waking up in the middle of the night.
Maintaining a regular night routine is an important part of sleeping well. More than 72% of Americans said they stick to a tried-and-true sleep regimen, with nearly 40% of Americans listening to music and 10% listening to audiobooks to wind down at bedtime
